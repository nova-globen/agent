Clean code is code that any developer can read and understand at a glance, without needing to decipher clever tricks or trace through layers of abstraction. It starts with meaningful names — variables, functions, and classes that reveal their intent so clearly that comments become unnecessary. Functions should do one thing and do it well, staying small enough to fit on a screen and focused enough that their name tells the whole story. Consistent formatting, minimal nesting, and the absence of duplication all contribute to a codebase that feels predictable and trustworthy. The payoff comes over time: clean code is easier to test, safer to refactor, and far less likely to hide subtle bugs. When maintainability is treated as a first-class concern from the start, the codebase becomes an asset rather than a liability, and new team members can contribute confidently without fear of breaking things they do not fully understand.
